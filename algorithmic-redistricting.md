---
title: "Algorithmic Redistricting"
slug: algorithmic-redistricting
summary: "Require all legislative district boundaries to be drawn by mathematical criteria with a mandatory compactness metric and partisan fairness test, eliminating human manipulation."
status: draft
order: 8
---

**Section 1.** Following each decennial census, all congressional and state legislative district boundaries shall be drawn in compliance with the following criteria, applied in strict order of priority:

1. Districts shall be as equal in population as practicable, using the most recent decennial census.
2. No district shall be drawn with the intent or effect of denying or abridging the right to vote on account of race, color, sex, or any other inherent characteristic of the electorate.
3. Every district shall be contiguous, such that every part of the district is reachable from every other part without crossing the district boundary.
4. Every district shall achieve a Polsby-Popper compactness score of no less than 0.25, and the statewide average compactness score shall be maximized.
5. District boundaries shall follow the boundaries of counties, municipalities, and tribal lands to the greatest extent consistent with the foregoing criteria.

**Section 2.** No district shall be drawn with the intent or effect of favoring or disfavoring any political party, incumbent, or candidate. The partisan fairness of each plan shall be measured by the efficiency gap. No plan shall be adopted if its efficiency gap exceeds eight percentage points in favor of any party.

**Section 3.** No data regarding partisan affiliation, voting history, or the residence of any incumbent or candidate may be used in the drawing of district boundaries. Partisan data shall be used solely to evaluate the completed plan against the efficiency gap standard in Section 2.

**Section 4.** The source data, methodology, and all proposed maps shall be published and made freely available to the public in sufficient detail that any person may independently verify compliance with Sections 1 through 3.

**Section 5.** No legislature, executive, or political body may draw or approve district boundaries. Each State shall establish or designate a nonpartisan body, independent of the legislature and executive, to draw districts in compliance with this Article. Members of, or candidates for, elected office, political party officers, and registered lobbyists are ineligible to serve on such body.

**Section 6.** This Article shall take effect and apply to the first general election held no fewer than one hundred days after the date of ratification, and to every election thereafter.
