---
title: "Algorithmic Redistricting"
slug: algorithmic-redistricting
summary: "Require all legislative district boundaries to be drawn by a publicly auditable mathematical algorithm, eliminating human manipulation."
status: draft
order: 8
---

**Section 1.** Following each decennial census, all congressional and state legislative district boundaries shall be determined by a mathematical algorithm using only census population data and geographic boundaries as inputs. No data regarding partisan affiliation, voting history, race, ethnicity, or the residence of any incumbent or candidate may be used.

**Section 2.** The algorithm shall place population-weighted seed points equal to the number of required districts, then iteratively assign geographic units to the nearest seed, adjusting boundaries to equalize population across districts while minimizing total boundary length. Districts must be contiguous. Where two boundary configurations yield equal total length, the configuration that preserves county, municipal, or tribal boundaries shall be preferred.

**Section 3.** The algorithm's source code, all inputs, and all outputs shall be published and freely available so that any person may independently verify the result. No legislature, commission, executive, or individual may alter the output.

**Section 4.** Congress shall establish, by law, the specific implementation within one year of ratification. If Congress fails to act, the most recent compliant algorithm shall be re-executed using new census data.
