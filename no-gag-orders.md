# No Gag Orders on Warrants Amendment

## Proposed Text

The right of the people to openly discuss all actions by the government being necessary for the protections of liberty; no warrant, subpoena, or other order targeting at least one Citizen shall be issued by any Local, State, Federal, or other court within the United States that contains any kind of order preventing the target or targets of said order, or third party persons, corporations, or other entities, from openly discussing that said warrant, subpoena, or other order has been issued or executed.

## Why This Amendment?

The use of gag orders — particularly National Security Letters (NSLs) and sealed court orders under the Foreign Intelligence Surveillance Act (FISA) — has created a system where the government can compel companies and individuals to hand over private data while simultaneously forbidding them from telling anyone it happened. This undermines the transparency that democratic accountability requires.

When a person or company cannot even acknowledge that a warrant exists, there is no mechanism for public scrutiny, no way to challenge overreach in the court of public opinion, and no path for affected individuals to seek support or counsel. The secrecy itself becomes a tool of control.

This amendment would not prevent law enforcement from executing lawful warrants — it would prevent them from hiding the fact that they did so. Transparency and accountability are not obstacles to justice; they are prerequisites for it.
