# Net Neutrality Amendment

## Proposed Text

Access to information generally, and the Internet in particular, being necessary for the protection of free expression, free thought, and free association; no corporation, person, government, or other entity shall, in any manner, throttle, filter, shape, or in any other way interfere with or alter information to or from any destination or source. With the reasonable exceptions of protecting hosts and services, with which one has a significant interest, from malicious attack or similar interference.

## Why This Amendment?

The Internet has become the primary medium through which people exercise their rights to free expression, free assembly, and access to information. Yet no constitutional protection prevents corporations or governments from selectively blocking, slowing, or manipulating the flow of information online.

Federal net neutrality rules have been adopted and repealed multiple times depending on the political composition of the FCC, creating an unstable regulatory environment that leaves the public's access to a free and open Internet subject to the priorities of whichever administration holds power. A constitutional amendment would remove this issue from the regulatory seesaw and establish equal access to information as a fundamental right.

Without structural protection, internet service providers can create "fast lanes" for those who pay more, throttle competitors, and shape what information reaches citizens — effectively creating a toll road on the marketplace of ideas.
