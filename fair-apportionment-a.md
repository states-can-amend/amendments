**Section 1.** No law shall fix or limit the total number of Representatives in the House of Representatives. The Permanent Apportionment Act of 1929, and any other law fixing or limiting the total number of Representatives, is repealed.

**Section 2.** Upon the official publication of each decennial census, the total number of Representatives shall equal the total resident population of the several States divided by the resident population of the least populous State, rounded to the nearest whole number. Seats shall be apportioned among the States by the method of equal proportions using the census figures. Each State shall be entitled to at least one Representative. This reapportionment is self-executing and requires no act of Congress.

**Section 3.** No Congress, State, or other body may alter, delay, or override the apportionment produced by the calculation in Section 2. Any candidate duly elected to a seat apportioned under this Article shall be seated.

**Section 4.** This Article shall take effect and apply to the first general election held no fewer than one hundred days after the date of ratification, and to every election thereafter.
