---
title: "Congressional Equal Application"
slug: congressional-equal-application
summary: "Ban Congress from passing laws that exempt themselves, and guarantee the people and the states the right to enforce it."
status: draft
order: 12
---

**Section 1.** Congress shall make no law, nor permit any regulation or policy, that grants to any member of Congress, officer of the United States, or employee of the legislative branch any right, privilege, benefit, or exemption not equally available to the citizens of the United States; nor shall Congress exempt any such person from any obligation, duty, or restriction imposed by law upon citizens generally.

**Section 2.** The right to enforce the provisions of this Article is reserved to the people and to the several States. Any citizen or State shall have standing to bring an action in Federal court to compel compliance with this Article. No congressional body shall have sole authority to adjudicate matters arising under this Article.

**Section 3.** Nothing in this Article shall be construed to abridge the protections of Article I, Section 6, as they pertain to speech or debate in either House.
