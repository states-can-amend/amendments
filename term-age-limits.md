---
title: "Term and Age Limits for Federal Office"
slug: term-age-limits
summary: "Set term limits for Congress and age limits for all federal elected officials and Supreme Court justices."
status: draft
order: 11
---

**Section 1.** No person shall serve more than six terms as a Representative in Congress, nor more than three terms as a Senator.

**Section 2.** No person who will have attained the age of seventy-five years on or before the scheduled date of inauguration for the office sought shall be eligible to file for, be appointed to, or assume any term of office as a Representative, Senator, President, or Vice President.

**Section 3.** No person shall be appointed to the office of Justice of the Supreme Court who shall not have attained the age of thirty-two years, nor any person who shall have attained the age of seventy years at the time of nomination. Any Justice who has attained the age of eighty years shall retire on or by the next July fourth following their eightieth birthday.

**Section 4.** Terms of office served prior to the ratification of this Article shall be counted toward the limits established in Section 1.

**Section 5.** This Article shall take effect beginning with the first general election following ratification.
