# Election Holidays Amendment

## Proposed Text

**Section 1.** All State, Local, and Federal elections shall happen on a holiday. If an election is to occur and it is not a holiday, it becomes a holiday for all compensation and wage calculation.

**Section 2.** For the purposes of employment or similar demands, the act of voting, and the time required to perform said act of voting, shall be guaranteed and exercising the right to vote shall be protected from all forms of retaliation.

## Why This Amendment?

The United States holds elections on a Tuesday — a workday — a tradition dating to 1845 when the agrarian calendar dictated the schedule. Today, this creates a structural barrier to voting for hourly workers, shift workers, and anyone who cannot afford to take time off.

While some states have adopted early voting, mail-in voting, or limited time-off-to-vote laws, these protections are inconsistent, incomplete, and revocable. A constitutional amendment would establish a nationwide floor: every election is a holiday, and no one can be punished for exercising the right to vote.

Section 2 goes further by explicitly protecting voters from retaliation. It is not enough to give someone time to vote if their employer can make them pay for it through reduced hours, poor assignments, or termination. The right to vote must be a protected activity, just as the right to organize is under labor law.
