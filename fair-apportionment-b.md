**Section 1.** No law shall fix or limit the total number of Representatives in the House of Representatives. The Permanent Apportionment Act of 1929, the Uniform Congressional District Act of 1967, and any other law fixing the number of Representatives or requiring single-member districts, is repealed.

**Section 2.** Upon the official publication of each decennial census, the total number of Representatives shall equal twice the result of dividing the total resident population of the several States by the resident population of the least populous State, rounded to the nearest whole number. Seats shall be apportioned among the States by the method of equal proportions using the census figures. Each State shall be entitled to at least one Representative. This reapportionment is self-executing and requires no act of Congress.

**Section 3.** Each State apportioned three or more Representatives shall elect them from multi-member districts of no fewer than three and no more than five Representatives each. States apportioned fewer than three Representatives shall elect them from single-member or at-large districts.

**Section 4.** All elections for Representatives shall use ranked choice voting. Voters shall rank candidates in order of preference. In multi-member districts, the candidates receiving the highest number of votes under the ranked choice tabulation, up to the number of seats in that district, shall be elected.

**Section 5.** No Congress, State, or other body may alter, delay, or override the apportionment produced by the calculation in Section 2 or the district size requirements of Section 3. Any candidate duly elected under this Article shall be seated.

**Section 6.** This Article shall take effect and apply to the first general election held no fewer than one hundred days after the date of ratification, and to every election thereafter.
